Samsung was founded on March 1, 1938. It was originally established as a trading company by Lee Byung-chul in Seoul, South Korea. Initially, it dealt primarily in groceries, dried-fish, noodles, and other goods, before gradually diversifying into different industries, including electronics, shipbuilding, construction, insurance, entertainment, and more, with electronics becoming one of its major focuses over the years. Samsung Group is a prominent global player known for its diverse range of products, technological innovations, and significant impact on various industries.

One unique aspect of Samsung is its deep involvement in multiple industries, which goes beyond its well-known consumer electronics division. Samsung not only manufactures smartphones, TVs, and home appliances but also plays a crucial role in sectors like shipbuilding, construction, insurance, and even theme parks and resorts. These aspects make Samsung a unique and multifaceted conglomerate that spans diverse industries, leads in innovation, and influences global technology trends.
